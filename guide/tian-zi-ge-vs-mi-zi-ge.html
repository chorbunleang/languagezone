田字格 vs 米字格: Which Grid to Use

田字格 and 米字格 are the two most common Chinese practice grids. Here's what each one does, and which to use as your handwriting improves.

If you have ever looked at a Chinese practice notebook, you have seen the faint grid printed in each square. That grid is not just for neatness — it is a learning tool that teaches your eye where each part of a character belongs. Here is what the common grid styles are, and when to use each one.

Why use grid paper at all?

Chinese characters are built from parts that must sit in the right proportion and position. A character written too wide, too tall, or unbalanced becomes hard to read. Grid paper gives you reference lines so you can place each stroke consistently, the same way lined paper helps with the height of English letters.

田字格 (tián zì gé)

The name means "field-character grid," because the square divided by a cross looks like the character 田 (field). A single vertical and horizontal line split the square into four equal quarters.

  • Best for: beginners, and for learning any new character.
  • Why: the cross shows you the centre, so you can balance the left and right halves and the top and bottom of a character.

This is the grid most learners should start with, and it is the default in our tool.

米字格 (mǐ zì gé)

The name means "rice-character grid," because the extra lines make the square resemble the character 米 (rice). It has the same cross as the 田字格 plus two diagonal lines, giving eight guide lines in total.

  • Best for: refining balance, and for complex characters with many strokes.
  • Why: the diagonals help you judge the angle and length of slanted strokes and keep dense characters from drifting off-centre.

Blank box (口字格)

A plain square with no inner lines.

  • Best for: more confident learners who want to test their sense of proportion without guide lines.
  • Why: once your eye is trained, writing inside a plain box is the closest step to writing freely on ordinary paper.

Which should you use?

  • Just starting out? Use the 田字格. The cross gives you the support you need.
  • Struggling with balance or a tricky character? Switch to the 米字格 for extra guidance.
  • Getting comfortable? Move to the blank box, then to plain paper.
A natural progression is 田字格 → 米字格 → blank box → plain paper. Move on when a character feels easy, and drop back a level whenever you tackle something new or complex.
