How to Start Learning Chinese

Chinese can feel overwhelming at first. This step-by-step roadmap breaks it into clear, manageable stages — from pinyin to your first characters and beyond.

Learning Chinese can feel overwhelming at the start — there's pronunciation, tones, thousands of characters, and no alphabet to fall back on. But with the right order of steps, it becomes a series of manageable, even enjoyable, stages. Here's a clear beginner's roadmap.

Step 1: Learn pinyin and the tones

Before characters, learn how Chinese sounds. Pinyin writes Mandarin pronunciation with the Latin alphabet, and the four tones give each syllable its meaning. Getting these right early prevents bad habits later. Start with our guide to pinyin and the four tones.

Step 2: Start writing basic characters

Handwriting is one of the most effective ways to remember characters — forming each stroke builds strong memory. Learn stroke order, understand the common radicals that make up characters, and practise on grid paper. Our free Worksheet Maker creates printable practice sheets for any characters you choose.

Step 3: Build core vocabulary

Focus on the most common characters and words first, since a small set covers most everyday Chinese. Work through the HSK 1 and HSK 2 characters, plus practical sets like numbers, family words, and colors.

Step 4: Practise listening and speaking

Reading and writing are only half the picture. Listen to simple Chinese audio and repeat out loud, focusing on tones. Speaking even a little from early on trains your ear and builds confidence.

Step 5: Build a daily habit

Consistency matters far more than long, occasional sessions. Ten to twenty focused minutes a day — a short worksheet, a few new words, some listening — adds up quickly over weeks and months.

A simple weekly rhythm: learn a handful of new characters, review older ones by writing them from memory, and spend a few minutes listening and repeating. Small, steady steps are what carry you to fluency.
