Common Chinese Radicals

Radicals (部首) are the building blocks of Chinese characters — recurring components that often hint at a character's meaning. Learning the common ones makes every new character easier to read, write, and remember.

Once you can spot radicals, characters stop looking random. Seeing 氵 tells you a character likely relates to water; 讠 relates to speech; 扌relates to the hand.

Why radicals matter

Chinese characters are not drawn at random — most are made of a small set of repeating parts. The radical is the key component used to categorise a character (and to look it up in a dictionary). Learning to write these common radicals cleanly gives you a huge head start on thousands of characters.
